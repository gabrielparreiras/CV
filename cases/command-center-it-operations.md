# Case: IT Command Center and Mission-Critical Operations

## Context

Large-scale technology operations require a structured command center model to coordinate monitoring, event management, incident response, crisis management, communication and service governance.

## Challenge

Improve operational control over critical services, reduce ambiguity during incidents and create a more mature model for event, incident, change and problem management.

Common challenges:

- Multiple teams involved in incident response.
- Fragmented monitoring and alerting.
- Lack of clear escalation criteria.
- Limited integration between technical events and business impact.
- Need for consistent communication during major incidents.

## Approach

The command center model was structured around operational governance, service management and observability.

Key practices:

- Defined operating processes for monitoring, event management and incident response.
- Integrated incident management, change management, problem management and crisis management routines.
- Established SLA/SLO-based visibility for critical services.
- Supported tooling selection and implementation for infrastructure monitoring and observability.
- Strengthened supplier governance for specialized monitoring and operational tools.
- Improved team routines and cross-functional communication.

## Business Impact

- Improved operational visibility and coordination across critical environments.
- Stronger incident response discipline.
- Better connection between monitoring, service levels and business impact.
- Increased maturity in 24x7 operations.

## Leadership Lessons

- A command center is not a room or dashboard; it is an operating model.
- Monitoring without decision rules creates noise. Monitoring connected to severity, ownership and business impact creates operational value.
- Incident response maturity depends on role clarity, communication discipline and post-incident learning.
