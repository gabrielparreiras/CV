# Case: Disaster Recovery, RTO and Cost Reduction

## Context

Mission-critical technology environments require disaster recovery capabilities that are both reliable and economically sustainable. Traditional DR models can become expensive, complex and operationally slow when they are not periodically revisited.

## Challenge

Reduce recovery time and operational cost while maintaining resilience, compliance expectations and continuity of critical services.

Typical constraints:

- High availability expectations.
- Regulatory and business continuity requirements.
- Complex infrastructure dependencies.
- Need to reduce manual execution during crisis scenarios.
- Need to improve RTO without increasing cost indefinitely.

## Approach

The initiative focused on revisiting the disaster recovery model, infrastructure activation process, automation potential and operational execution flow.

Key practices:

- Reviewed infrastructure activation strategy for DR scenarios.
- Identified manual steps, bottlenecks and cost drivers.
- Improved operational readiness and recovery execution.
- Connected technical recovery practices with business continuity requirements.
- Created measurable improvement opportunities around RTO and cost.

## Business Impact

- Estimated saving of R$25MM through cost and RTO reduction in a disaster recovery environment.
- Recognition as finalist in a corporate continuous improvement award.
- Improved executive visibility of the relationship between resilience, cost and business continuity.

## Leadership Lessons

- Disaster recovery must be treated as an engineering, operational and financial discipline.
- RTO reduction should be pursued through architecture, automation, process clarity and periodic testing.
- Cost optimization in DR cannot compromise continuity risk; decisions must be explicit and risk-based.
