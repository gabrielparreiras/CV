# Case: SRE and Observability for Critical Digital Platforms

## Context

Critical digital platforms operating in a highly regulated financial environment, with dependency on internal applications, cloud components, SaaS integrations, customer-facing journeys and high availability requirements.

## Challenge

Improve reliability, customer experience, availability governance and operational visibility across complex digital journeys while reducing change risk and accelerating engineering learning cycles.

Typical symptoms in this kind of environment include:

- Fragmented visibility between backend, frontend and SaaS providers.
- Operational indicators not fully connected to customer experience.
- High dependency on incident response instead of preventive engineering.
- Need for better prioritization of technical debt, reliability debt and risk mitigation.
- Need to connect architecture, product, engineering and operations under shared reliability goals.

## Approach

The operating approach combined engineering governance, observability, SRE practices and business indicators.

Key initiatives:

- Defined and evolved SLA/SLO governance for critical applications and provider integrations.
- Applied Golden Signals and operational telemetry to improve visibility of latency, traffic, errors and saturation.
- Strengthened MTTR, availability, capacity, performance and change indicators.
- Connected observability with customer journeys, including front-end behavior, errors, abandonments and digital experience indicators.
- Used modern deployment strategies such as feature flags, canary releases, blue-green deployment and progressive delivery.
- Introduced failure analysis and continuous improvement practices to reduce recurrence.
- Used DORA metrics, ITIL, Well-Architected Framework and technical debt indicators as part of management routines.

## Business and Technical Impact

- Better alignment between engineering, product, architecture and operations.
- Improved ability to detect, prioritize and mitigate customer-impacting failures.
- Reduced operational risk in critical changes.
- Increased reliability governance maturity.
- Contribution to improved digital customer experience indicators, including +25 NPS points and app store rating of 4.9 in strategic applications.

## Leadership Lessons

- SRE cannot be treated as an isolated production function; it must influence ideation, architecture, delivery, operations and deprecation.
- Reliability indicators must be understandable both by engineering teams and business stakeholders.
- Observability only creates value when connected to decisions, prioritization and customer impact.
- Error budgets and technical debt must be managed as business risk, not only engineering backlog.
