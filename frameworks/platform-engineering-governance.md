# Framework: Platform Engineering Governance

## Purpose

Define governance for platform engineering initiatives that support developer productivity, reliability, security, cloud efficiency and operational excellence.

---

## Platform Engineering Objectives

- Accelerate delivery through reusable capabilities.
- Reduce cognitive load for engineering teams.
- Standardize secure and reliable patterns.
- Improve observability and operability by default.
- Enable self-service with guardrails.
- Improve cost, compliance and operational control.

---

## Governance Domains

| Domain | Governance Questions |
|---|---|
| Product Thinking | Is the platform managed as a product? |
| Developer Experience | Does it reduce friction for engineering teams? |
| Reliability | Are standards aligned with SRE practices? |
| Security | Are secure defaults embedded? |
| Cloud | Are architecture and cost guardrails available? |
| Observability | Is telemetry standardized? |
| Automation | Are workflows repeatable and self-service? |
| Metrics | Are adoption, productivity and reliability measured? |

---

## Platform Scorecard

| Metric | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Platform Adoption | Measures internal product usage |
| Lead Time Reduction | Measures delivery acceleration |
| Self-Service Ratio | Measures autonomy |
| Golden Path Coverage | Measures standardization |
| Change Failure Rate | Measures delivery quality |
| SLO Compliance | Measures reliability |
| Cost Allocation Compliance | Measures FinOps maturity |
| Developer Satisfaction | Measures platform value |

---

## Operating Cadence

- Weekly: backlog, adoption blockers and critical incidents.
- Monthly: platform metrics, reliability, cost and developer feedback.
- Quarterly: roadmap, maturity, investment and executive alignment.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Platform as infrastructure ticket factory.
- Building tools without internal product management.
- Enforcing standards without developer experience.
- Measuring activity instead of adoption and value.
- Ignoring cost and operational implications.
