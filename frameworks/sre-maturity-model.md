# Framework: SRE Maturity Model

## Purpose

Assess the maturity of Site Reliability Engineering practices across services, teams and platforms.

---

## Maturity Levels

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Reactive | Incidents are handled mainly through manual response and heroics |
| 2 | Visible | Monitoring exists, but actionability and ownership are inconsistent |
| 3 | Managed | SLOs, incident process and ownership are defined for critical services |
| 4 | Integrated | Reliability is part of architecture, delivery and prioritization decisions |
| 5 | Optimized | Reliability is continuously improved through automation, error budgets and learning loops |

---

## Assessment Dimensions

| Dimension | Questions |
|---|---|
| Ownership | Does every critical service have a clear owner? |
| SLIs/SLOs | Are service indicators and objectives defined and reviewed? |
| Observability | Are telemetry signals actionable and tied to customer impact? |
| Incident Management | Are roles, severities and communication standards defined? |
| Post-Incident Review | Are incidents converted into backlog and prevention actions? |
| Automation | Is toil measured and reduced? |
| Change Risk | Are deployments progressive and reversible? |
| Capacity | Are performance and saturation managed proactively? |
| Executive Governance | Are reliability risks visible to leadership? |

---

## Scoring Guide

Use a 1 to 5 score for each dimension:

1. Ad hoc or absent.
2. Partially defined.
3. Defined and used for critical services.
4. Integrated into delivery and governance routines.
5. Continuously optimized and automated.

---

## Output

The assessment should produce:

- Current maturity score.
- Top reliability risks.
- Critical service gaps.
- Priority improvement backlog.
- Executive summary.
- 90-day action plan.
